The Power of Promise in 2026

The last year has laid bare how inequity continues to shape the environments where Chicagoland children grow up. These realities show up every day—in classrooms, in communities, and in futures constrained too early. And still, our young people remain hopeful. They have not given up on their futures, and neither can we.

This moment calls for leadership that acts early, invests wisely, and listens closely to community voice—because the conditions shaping young people’s lives today determine the outcomes we see tomorrow. Together with our cohort of 22 community-based organizations and a diverse network of partners and experts, Bright Promises continues to evolve to respond to urgent needs while building toward long-term change.

In the pages that follow, The Power of Promise explores the critical space between environments and outcomes—the “missing middle” where development happens. This report shows what becomes possible when communities, partners, and donors come together to build the conditions young people need to thrive.

As you read, we invite you to see how acting early—together—protects opportunity and builds the future our children deserve.

In Solidarity, Katherine Korey Executive Director
Bright Promises Foundation

The Inflection Point

Key Insight 1: Not all children start with the same opportunities—and the gap is widening.

Across Chicagoland, children and families are navigating fractured supports and rising stress and trauma. In partnership with community-based organizations and donors, Bright Promises helps children get the resources, guidance, and skills they deserve to grow, learn, and thrive.

Photography

Two students in a warm community setting; outdoor or school backdrop conveying belonging and possibility

As the opportunity gap widens across Chicagoland, the organizations closest to the communities that need them most are being asked to do more with dramatically less. Rising stress, trauma, and fractured systems have increased demand for their services—just as funding has become more volatile and uncertain.

Our community-based partners are deeply trusted. Especially by young people and their families, they are often the first place families turn, the most responsive in moments of crisis, and the most agile in adapting to changing community needs.

For donors, this means your investment stabilizes trusted community anchors—so they can keep showing up when families need them most.

Photography

Community convening or small group of youth and adults in an authentic, candid moment of connection

Bright Promises:

  • Anticipates emerging needs early
  • Invests where larger institutions can’t or won’t
  • Builds capacity from the inside out
  • Creates cross-sector alignment where silos normally exist
  • Elevates community leadership
  • Influences public systems and policy

Bright Promises ensures these trusted partners can continue to lead. Through multi-year investment, capacity-building, and leadership development, we help stabilize the organizations that anchor opportunity—so communities can keep showing up for children when it matters most.

Community of Partners

Change doesn’t happen in isolation.

It happens when leaders come together with shared purpose and a commitment to act. Bright Promises is proud to partner with these organizations—innovators and changemakers working alongside families and communities most impacted by inequity.

Together, these organizations represent more than $167 million in annual operating resources across Chicagoland—reflecting the scale, reach, and collective capacity of this network. This community of leaders show up for kids every day—with creativity, courage, and deep commitment.

Through multi-year communities of practice, our partners learn from one another, share data and insight, and coordinate action across systems that too often work in silos.

Momentum, Not Miracles

Key Insight 2: When young people have safe spaces, trusted relationships, and opportunities to lead, they don’t just cope—they thrive.

Bright Promises pilots initiatives that deliver measurable near and long-term outcomes for over 11,000 youth and families each year. From increased confidence and agency to reduced violence and improved wellbeing, these outcomes don’t happen by chance—they happen through building the conditions young people need to succeed.

Photography

Youth in a safe, affirming space with a trusted adult mentor; genuine relationship visible

Our approach centers what systems too often underfund: safe spaces, trusted relationships, and meaningful opportunities for young people to lead.

Through Bright Promises, donors help pilot and scale solutions that deliver measurable near- and long-term outcomes for over 11,000 youth and families each year. Since 2016, Bright Promises has invested over $5.6 million in 100 community-based organizations serving low-income children and families across Chicagoland and Illinois.

Photography

Youth leadership moment — young person facilitating, presenting, or leading peers in a structured setting

Our investments translate into real results for kids: increased confidence and agency, stronger relationships, improved wellbeing, and reduced risky behaviors. By strengthening the conditions young people need to succeed, Bright Promises and our partners reduce the need for more costly downstream responses—such as emergency intervention or lost educational and economic opportunity.

We’ve learned that when communities are trusted and supported, these changes are not only possible—they’re repeatable.

  • Youth experience increased safety, belonging, confidence, and agency
  • Frontline organizations adopt lasting trauma-informed and SEL policies
  • Improved staff retention and reduced burnout
  • Strengthened relationships across sector
  • Higher youth engagement in school, community, and civic action
  • Fewer discipline incidents and justice contacts
  • Youth shape community planning and public narratives
  • Stronger, coordinated local ecosystems
  • Innovations spread across agencies via train-the-trainer replication
  • More equitable, trauma-informed, stable public systems
  • Scaled statewide practices
  • Reduced violence, improved mental health, stronger workforce pipelines
  • Thriving, resilient communities where youth shape the future
Impact Spotlight

Healing Youth Racial Trauma

Since launching the Healing, Leading, Changing initiative in 2021, Bright Promises has invested more than $2 million to create and scale programs that confront racial trauma and build youth leadership across Chicagoland.

Healing racial trauma and developing youth leadership are essential—not optional—for academic success, economic mobility, and participatory democracy. This initiative supports young people in building confidence, agency, identity, and leadership skills, laying the groundwork for long-term success in school, work, and life.

We Invest in the People and Places Children Rely On

Key Insight 3: Strong outcomes for children start with supported adults and organizations.

When educators and caregivers have the resources, training, and stability they need, children experience more consistent care, stronger relationships, and healthier environments. With your support, Bright Promises helps youth-serving professionals build skills, reduce burnout, and stay in the field—leading to more stable classrooms and stronger programs.

Quality Care and Learning Starts with Quality Jobs

Bright Promises helps build the strong, skilled workforce that children and families rely on every day.

  • 2,200+ frontline professionals engaged across early childhood, K–12, afterschool, early intervention, and behavioral health since 2008.
  • Train-the-trainer models that multiply skills and practices across organizations and sectors.
  • Multi-year, cross-sector cohorts that strengthen communication, alignment, and shared language among systems that rarely collaborate.

The result: more stable teams, stronger programs, and better coordinated support for kids and families across Chicagoland.

Key Insight 5

Scaling what works through trust, partnership, and community leadership creates lasting opportunity for children.

Lasting systems change doesn’t come from top-down replication alone. It happens when proven ideas are grown from the ground up—rooted in community trust, strengthened through shared learning, and supported by flexible, multi-year investment.

By combining the risk-tolerance of venture philanthropy with the relationship-building and movement power of grassroots organizing, Bright Promises helps community-based organizations scale what works while staying responsive to the people they serve.

The result is a stronger, more coordinated ecosystem where effective practices spread, organizations grow together, and children benefit from opportunity that lasts.

Our Goals

These commitments reflect what our community has told us matters most—now and for the future.

Embed trauma-informed and SEL practices

Into organizational policy, leadership, and daily practice across community-based organizations throughout Chicagoland—so care, safety, and belonging are built into the systems children experience every day.

Strengthen workforce stability and resilience

By reducing burnout, supporting retention, and investing in the people who carry this work forward, even under extraordinary strain.

Build shared infrastructure for scale and influence

Enabling tools, learning, and promising practices to spread beyond individual grantees and drive broader systems uptake.

If you believe children deserve more—and that communities already hold the answers—we invite you to keep building this future with us.

Corporate Partner: West Monroe

West Monroe’s long-standing partnership with Bright Promises goes beyond volunteerism.

In the communities we serve, increased ICE activity has intensified fear and uncertainty—causing anxiety, trauma, and chronic stress. Mental health kits assembled by West Monroe volunteers are a critical part of Bright Promises’ response. Distributed through trusted community partners, these kits connect families to free, trauma-informed SEL resources through the SEL Access Exchange.

With demand far exceeding staff capacity, partnerships like this are essential—enabling Bright Promises to meet urgent need and respond with care during moments of crisis.
